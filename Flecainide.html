FLECAINIDE


Flecainide is a medication that belongs to the class of antiarrhythmic drugs. It is primarily used to treat certain types of irregular heart rhythms, specifically ventricular arrhythmias and atrial fibrillation/flutter. Here are some key points to know about flecainide: Mechanism of action: Flecainide works by slowing down the electrical signals in the heart, thereby restoring normal rhythm. It specifically targets the sodium channels in the heart muscle cells, helping to stabilize the heart's electrical activity. Usage: Flecainide is prescribed for the treatment and prevention of various abnormal heart rhythms, including ventricular tachycardia, ventricular fibrillation, and certain types of atrial fibrillation/flutter. It is typically used in individuals who have a structurally normal heart or specific heart conditions that are known to respond well to this medication. Dosage: The dosage of flecainide can vary depending on individual factors, such as the type and severity of the arrhythmia and the patient's response to the medication. It is important to follow the dosage instructions provided by your healthcare professional, as well as any adjustments they may make during treatment. .
