KALETRA


Kaletra is a brand name for a medication that combines two active ingredients: lopinavir and ritonavir. It is primarily used in the treatment of human immunodeficiency virus (HIV) infection. The tablet form of Kaletra contains a fixed-dose combination of lopinavir and ritonavir. The exact appearance of Kaletra tablets can vary depending on the manufacturer and the specific formulation. Generally, Kaletra tablets are oval-shaped and come in different strengths, typically 100 mg of lopinavir and 25 mg of ritonavir per tablet. They are film-coated for easy swallowing. .
