Ropinirole


Ropinirole is one of several medications known as dopamine agonists that are used to manage the symptoms of Parkinson's disease. Parkinson's disease is a neurological disorder characterized by the degeneration of dopamine-producing cells in the brain. Ropinirole helps to replace dopamine and stimulate dopamine receptors, thereby alleviating motor symptoms such as tremors, stiffness, and difficulty with movement.
